Soupe Kandia

Soupe Kandia : One of the best senegalese dish

Description

Soupe Kandia is a traditional Senegalese dish that is made with a variety of vegetables, meat, and fish. The dish is typically made with okra, eggplant, carrots, cabbage, onions, garlic, and tomatoes. The meat used in the dish can be beef, lamb, or goat, while the fish can be any type of fish that is available.

The dish is cooked in a large pot, with the vegetables and meat being saut√©ed in oil before being simmered in water. The fish is typically added towards the end of the cooking process to prevent it from overcooking. The dish is seasoned with a variety of spices, including black pepper, curmin and coriander.

Soupe Kandia is typically served with rice or couscous, and is often accompanied by a spicy sauce made from chili peppers, onions, and tomatoes. The dish is popular throughout Senegal and is often served at special occasions and celebrations. It is also a popular street food in Senegal, with vendors selling the dish from large pots on the side of the road.

Ingredients

  • Okra
  • Eggplant
  • Carrots
  • Cabbage
  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • Tomatoes
  • Meat (beef, lamb, or goat)
  • Fish
  • Oil
  • Black pepper
  • Cumin
  • Coriander
  • Salt
  • Water

Steps

  1. Wash the okra
  2. Cut off both ends and cut it into slices. Put in a bowl and add a lot of water
  3. Wash okra two more times to rinse off the stickiness
  4. Clean the fish and cut it into 3 parts
  5. Prepare the rice, rinse and cook it
  6. Put the 0.75 litres of water to heat in a pot
  7. Add beef and pieces of fish
  8. Add the okra and cook for 15 minutes
  9. Remove the fish
  10. Cut the meat into medium-sized pieces and add to okra, along with chopped onion and garlic
  11. Add a pinch of pepper, salt, bouillon cubes, palm oil and fresh chili peppers. Mix it well and cook for 15 minutes
  12. Return the fish to the pot and cook for another 10 minutes
  13. Serve hot with rice
